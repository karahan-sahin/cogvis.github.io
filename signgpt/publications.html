Programme Outputs

Research Papers

Google Scholar Official list
2025
Geo-Sign: Hyperbolic Contrastive Regularisation for Geometrically Aware Sign Language Translation
E. Fish, R. Bowden
NeurIPS 2025 Translation

Introduces hyperbolic geometry as an inductive bias for sign language translation, showing that contrastive regularisation in a Poincaré disk space captures the hierarchical structure of sign language semantics and improves translation quality across four benchmarks.

PDF Code
Gloss-Free Sign Language Translation: An Unbiased Evaluation of Progress in the Field
O. Mercanoglu Sincan, J.H. Low, S. Asasi, R. Bowden
CVIU 2025 Translation

A systematic, protocol-controlled benchmarking study of gloss-free sign language translation methods, identifying sources of variance in reported results and establishing standardised evaluation conditions for reproducible comparison.

PDF
2026
SignAgent: Agentic LLMs for Linguistically-Grounded Sign Language Annotation and Dataset Curation
O. Cory, O.M. Sincan, R. Bowden
arXiv 2026 Agentic / LLMs

Proposes an agentic LLM pipeline for semi-automatic annotation of sign language corpora, grounded in BSL linguistic theory. Demonstrates substantial reductions in annotation cost while maintaining agreement with expert annotators on phonological and semantic labels.

arXiv
M3T: Discrete Multi-Modal Motion Tokens for Sign Language Production
A. Symeonidis-Herzig, J. Low, O.M. Sincan, R. Bowden
arXiv 2026 Production

Introduces a discrete multi-modal codebook for representing sign language motion, enabling high-quality avatar-based sign production via a two-stage tokenise-then-generate pipeline. Outperforms continuous motion regression baselines on naturalness metrics.

arXiv
SignSparK: Efficient Multilingual Sign Language Production via Sparse Keyframe Learning
J. Low, A. Symeonidis-Herzig, M. Ivashechkin, O.M. Sincan, R. Bowden
arXiv 2026 Production

A sparse keyframe approach to sign language production that achieves competitive quality at a fraction of the computational cost of dense generation. Demonstrates zero-shot transfer across BSL, DGS, and ASL.
