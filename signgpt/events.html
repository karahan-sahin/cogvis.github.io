Resources

Open tools, educational materials, and lexical resources produced by the SignGPT programme, freely available to the research community and the public.

Software Toolkit
Visual Language Toolkit (VLT)

An open-source Python toolkit for processing and analysing sign language video. Components include hand and body tracking, face analysis (expression, gaze, mouthing), phonological parameter extraction, and sign spotting, all built on the SignGPT visual backbone.

Release: forthcoming
Educational Resource
AI Glossary of Terms in BSL

A community-developed, expert-reviewed glossary of artificial intelligence and computing terminology translated into British Sign Language. Co-created with Deaf advisors to support accessible communication about AI research and to establish agreed signs for emerging technical vocabulary.

View on official site →
Explainer
What is AI? — English & BSL

Bilingual explainer content presenting key concepts in artificial intelligence in both English and British Sign Language. Aimed at broadening AI literacy among Deaf audiences and demonstrating the project's commitment to accessible public engagement.

View on official site →
Dataset
BSL Video Corpus (v1)

A large-scale, multi-source BSL video corpus collected under the programme. Includes broadcast material, educational video, and community-contributed recordings, with partial phonological and semantic annotation. Largest publicly available annotated BSL corpus.

Release: forthcoming
Benchmark
BSL Translation Benchmark

Standardised test sets and evaluation scripts for continuous BSL-to-English translation, with linguistically informed metrics that go beyond BLEU — including spatial grammar adequacy and non-manual feature retention scores.

Release: forthcoming
Code
SignGPT Model Weights & Code

Pre-trained model weights, training scripts, and inference code for the SignGPT transformer, to be released progressively as papers are published under open-source licences on GitHub.

Public Engagement

Outreach & Community Engagement

The SignGPT programme has a strong commitment to public engagement and to keeping the Deaf community at the centre of its work. This means going beyond passive consultation: Deaf advisors and community organisation partners are involved in research design, and our public engagement activities are co-created with Deaf presenters and communicators.

We run regular public events — including school visits, science fairs, and Deaf community open days — to explain the research and gather community input. All public-facing materials from the programme are produced bilingually in English and BSL. The AI Glossary and "What is AI?" explainer are examples of this bilingual-first approach.

If you would like to invite a member of the SignGPT team to speak at your event, or would like to partner with the programme on community engagement activities, please contact Dr. Steven Emery (Programme Manager) at s.emery@surrey.ac.uk.
