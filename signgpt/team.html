Programme Leadership

Principal Investigators

RB

Prof. Richard Bowden

Lead Principal Investigator  ·  CVSSP, University of Surrey

Richard Bowden is Professor of Computer Vision and Machine Learning at the Centre for Vision, Speech and Signal Processing (CVSSP), University of Surrey, where he has worked for over two decades on sign language recognition and production. He is a leading authority on human motion analysis, gesture recognition, and vision-language modelling for sign languages. His group has published extensively at top venues including CVPR, ICCV, ECCV, NeurIPS, and ACL, and he leads the CogVis Laboratory.

Sign Language Recognition Sign Language Production Human Motion Analysis Video Understanding Vision–Language Models

AZ

Prof. Andrew Zisserman

Principal Investigator  ·  VGG, University of Oxford

Andrew Zisserman is Professor of Computer Vision Engineering at the University of Oxford, where he leads the Visual Geometry Group (VGG). He is one of the most highly cited researchers in computer vision, known for foundational contributions to visual recognition, 3D reconstruction, and large-scale video understanding. Within SignGPT he leads the large-scale pre-training and model scaling workstreams.

Large-Scale Visual Pre-Training Video Understanding Visual Recognition Self-Supervised Learning
Oxford Profile
KC

Prof. Kearsy Cormier

Principal Investigator  ·  DCAL, University College London

Kearsy Cormier is Professor of Sign Language Linguistics at the Deafness, Cognition and Language Research Centre (DCAL), UCL. Her research focuses on the grammar of BSL, with particular expertise in classifier predicates, discourse structure, and the cross-linguistic study of sign languages. Within SignGPT she leads the Pillar 3 linguistics integration workstream and co-leads dataset annotation.

BSL Grammar Classifier Predicates Discourse Structure Cross-Linguistic Sign Language Research
UCL DCAL
BW

Prof. Bencie Woll

Principal Investigator  ·  DCAL, University College London

Bencie Woll is Emeritus Professor of Sign Language and Deaf Studies at DCAL, UCL, and one of the world's foremost authorities on BSL and Deaf communities. She has conducted landmark longitudinal studies of BSL acquisition, change, and endangerment, and advises government and public bodies on BSL policy. Within SignGPT she provides linguistic and community oversight across all four pillars.

BSL Language Acquisition Language Documentation Deaf Community Studies BSL Policy
